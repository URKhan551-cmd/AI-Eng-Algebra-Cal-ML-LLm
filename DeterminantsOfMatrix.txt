Determinants are one of the most 
important concepts in Linear Algebra. 
They tell us whether a matrix is invertible,
 how much a transformation stretches or shrinks space, and whether vectors are linearly independent.


 DETERMINANT :: is a single number compute or calculated from  a ssquare matrix.

 A = | a b |
     | c d |    detA = ad - bc 

     A= | 4 3 |
        | 2 5 |     detA = 4x5 - 3x2 => 20 - 6 = 14.    this is out detA 14


        Geometric Meaning

This is the most important intuition.

Imagine the two column vectors

[4  2],  [3  5]
They form a parallelogram.

The determinant equals the signed area of that parallelogram.

determinant provide critical insights into the property of matrix A.
